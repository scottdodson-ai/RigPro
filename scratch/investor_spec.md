**2. How Pre-Revenue SaaS Valuation Works**

At the prototype + LOI stage, there is no traditional revenue-based
valuation. Instead, investors use three primary frameworks, each
producing a different estimate. The final valuation is typically
negotiated within the range these frameworks define.

**Framework 1 --- Future ARR Discounted Back**

Investors estimate realistic ARR (annual recurring revenue) 2--3 years
out, apply a SaaS multiple (8--15× for construction tech), then discount
70--85% for execution risk and time value.

  ------------------------------ ----------------------------- ---------------------- ------------------- -----------------------
  **Scenario**                   **Projected ARR (Yr 2--3)**   **Revenue Multiple**   **Risk Discount**   **Implied Valuation**
  Rigging-only moderate          \$2.15M (Yr 2)                10×                    75%                 \~\$5.4M
  Rigging + expansion moderate   \$6.46M (Yr 3)                10×                    75%                 \~\$16.2M
  Conservative rigging-only      \$717K (Yr 2)                 8×                     75%                 \~\$1.4M
  ------------------------------ ----------------------------- ---------------------- ------------------- -----------------------

**Framework 2 --- Berkus Method**

Standard for pre-revenue startups. Assigns up to \$500K--\$2M of value
to each of five risk-reduction factors that have been addressed:

  -------------------------------- ---------------- ------------------ --------------------------------------------
  **Risk Reducer**                 **Status**       **Value Range**    **Notes**
  Sound idea / validated concept   ✓ Confirmed      \$500K -- \$1M     Niche underserved by all major platforms
  Working prototype                ✓ Demonstrated   \$1M -- \$2M       Full quote-to-calendar workflow live
  Quality management team          Unknown          \$0 -- \$1M        Industry experience adds significant value
  Strategic relationships / LOIs   ✓ In hand        \$500K -- \$1.5M   Proof of willingness-to-pay
  Sales / product rollout          Not yet          \$0                Will add value at next stage
  TOTAL BERKUS RANGE                                \$2M -- \$5.5M     
  -------------------------------- ---------------- ------------------ --------------------------------------------

**4. What Pushes RigPro3\'s Valuation to the Higher End**

Several specific factors in RigPro3\'s profile support a premium
relative to a generic pre-revenue prototype. These are the points to
emphasize in investor conversations:

-   **Crane & rigging specificity:** Defensible niche

The market is underserved and WrightPlan, the closest competitor, has
weak UI, no modern estimating logic, and dated architecture. Niche SaaS
with a clear incumbent to displace is one of the most fundable investor
narratives.

-   **Working prototype:** Proof of concept, not vaporware

The full quote-to-calendar workflow eliminates technology risk --- the
\#1 concern at seed stage. Most seed pitches are decks only; you have a
live demo.

-   **LOIs in hand:** Willingness-to-pay validated

Even 1--2 signed LOIs from rigging companies is worth more than 100 warm
leads. It is the difference between \'people might pay for this\' and
\'people have committed to pay for this.\'

-   **Expansion roadmap:** Path to \$500M+ market, not just \$7M

The 4-phase expansion from rigging to millwright to specialty
contractors to general contractors shows investors a path from a
defensible niche into a \$900M+ addressable market. Investors fund the
trajectory, not just the starting point.

-   **Procore/Buildertrend won\'t compete:** No BigCo threat

The rigging market is too small for Procore (\$1.15B revenue) or
Buildertrend (\~\$500M revenue) to prioritize. This is actually a
positive --- investors know you won\'t get crushed by a platform player
before you reach scale.

-   **Unique IP:** 7 features no competitor has

Customer-specific labor rate overrides, live net profit slider,
equipment conflict detection, per-associate pipeline analytics, and the
inbound request-to-estimate workflow are features that exist nowhere
else in this competitive set. That\'s a moat.
